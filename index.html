Frequently Asked Questions

Halal Scanner uses advanced OCR and a comprehensive database to provide highly accurate results. However, we always recommend consulting with a qualified Islamic scholar for definitive rulings, especially for complex cases. Our system learns and improves continuously based on user feedback and updates to halal certification standards.
Currently, AI Halal Scanner supports English, Arabic, French, German, Turkish, and Indonesian. We're continuously working to add more languages. The app can recognize ingredient lists in these languages and provide analysis in your preferred language.
For ingredients classified as mashbooh (doubtful), we provide detailed explanations about why they might be questionable and list alternative names they might appear under. We also indicate when further research or consultation with a scholar might be needed. Users can submit feedback if they have additional information about specific ingredients.
For guest users, scan results are only stored temporarily in your browser and are not saved to our servers. If you create an account, you can choose to save your scan history for future reference. We never share your personal data with third parties. All images are processed securely and deleted after analysis.

Important Disclaimer

Halal Scanner provides information and guidance about food ingredients based on our extensive database and OCR analysis. However, we cannot guarantee 100% accuracy for several reasons:

  • Manufacturers may change formulations without notice
  • Ingredient sources may vary by region or production batch
  • Islamic scholars may differ in their interpretations
  • Some ingredients require specific knowledge of their production process

For definitive rulings, especially in matters of personal religious observance, we strongly recommend consulting with qualified Islamic scholars or halal certification bodies in your area.

By using Halal Scanner, you acknowledge that the results are for informational purposes only and should not be considered as a fatwa or definitive religious ruling.
